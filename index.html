Developing a link to fast and sustainable travel.

Hyperlink is London’s first student team focused on developing effective Hyperloop technology. It was founded in September 2020 and operates from Queen Mary University of London.


The team consists of 42 members from 3 different universities - Queen Mary University of London, Imperial College London and University College London.


Our vision is to accelerate the implementation of the Hyperloop system in Europe. We aim to achieve it through attending international competitions as well as researching the engineering and economic viability of different concepts.

With over 40 members from an extensive range of ethnic and cultural backgrounds, our Hyperloop team comprises 12 nationalities, across 5 continents and a total of 20 different languages spoken.


There are 11 different courses studied by Hyperlink members making us a highly interdisciplinary team with extended knowledge on a broad number of topics.

Hyperloop is the proposed high-speed passenger and freight transportation system, consisting of a network of near-vacuum tubes and levitating pods. Due to virtually no friction or drag being present, the vehicles can travel up to 1200 km/h and maintain a much greater energetic efficiency than airplanes.


Hyperloop is going to be the most effective on medium length routes, such as London to Edinburgh.

We are developing and building a fully operational Hyperloop pod
We are researching different aspects of the system, such as making it energy-efficient and safe for humans
We are creating a truly London-wide initiative by uniting the city’s best universities and most ambitious students

Hyperlink is set on designing and constructing the best possible pod and help to improve the technical viability of the Hyperloop technology. The support of our partners and sponsors are essential for us to achieve our goals. In return for sponsorship we are offering various advertising opportunities and the possibility to be in touch with students of academic excellence, the leading engineers of tomorrow.
